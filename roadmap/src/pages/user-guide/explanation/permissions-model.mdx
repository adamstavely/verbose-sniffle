Permissions are deliberately simple: access is granted by **workspace
membership** and shaped by a **role**.

## Membership grants access

If you're a member of a workspace, you can see its shared content by default.
There's no per-item approval queue, because within a team that friction usually
costs more than it protects.

## Roles shape what you can change

Roles (Member, Admin) decide who can manage people and settings. Keeping the set
of roles small makes the model easy to reason about — you rarely have to guess
who can do what.

## The trade-off

This model favors collaboration over fine-grained control. For teams that need
stricter boundaries, separate workspaces are the recommended tool rather than
deep per-item permissions.
